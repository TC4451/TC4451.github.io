I am currently a graduate student in Computational Science and Engineering at [Harvard University](https://www.harvard.edu/), supported by the Amazon Robotics Day One Fellowship. I conduct research on world models and VLAs under the supervision of [Prof. Yilun Du](https://yilundu.github.io/).

Previously, I received my B.S. in Computer Science from [Worcester Polytechnic Institute](https://www.wpi.edu/) (WPI), where I worked as a research assistant at [VISLab@WPI](https://zhang-vislab.github.io/) under the supervision of [Prof. Ziming Zhang](https://www.wpi.edu/people/faculty/zzhang15). I was also a research student at [Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center](https://www.bidmc.org/) under the supervision of [Prof. Feng Tian](https://www.fengtianlab.com/) and summer research intern at [Carnegie Mellon University Robotic Institute](https://www.ri.cmu.edu/) under supervision of [Prof. Howie Choset](https://www.ri.cmu.edu/ri-faculty/howie-choset/). Earlier, I worked at the [Robotic Materials Group](https://wp.wpi.edu/roboticmaterialsgroup/) at WPI under the supervision of [Prof. Markus Nemitz](https://www.wpi.edu/people/faculty/mnemitz), and at the [Learning Sciences & Technologies Center](https://www.wpi.edu/academics/departments/learning-sciences-technologies) with [Prof. Jacob R. Whitehill](https://www.wpi.edu/people/faculty/jrwhitehill).

My research interests include foundation model pretraining, efficient retrieval for large-scale datasets, and robot learning. I am particularly interested in combining world models with large foundation models to enable generalizable, efficient behavior and real-world deployment on robotic and autonomous systems.
